Honorable Mentions


Steph Curry

With his impact on the people, especially youngsters of basketball, Steph Curry has earned a spot on the honorable mentions list. The best shooter of ALL TIME, record for most 3-pointers scored ALL-TIME, 4-time NBA Champion, 2-time NBA MVP, and 1-time Finals MVP winner.

Wilt Chamberlain

During the first part of Wilt's career, NBA game statistics weren't recorded. However, he still managed to bring out amazing performances throughout his time. Wilt holds the record for most points scored in an NBA game with 100. It was also said that he would score 50 points a game on average. He has only won two NBA championships in his time but he has done a lot which got him to be in this list.

Shaquille O'Neal

One of the most dominant big men in the NBA, breaking many backboards and rims throughout his career, Shaquille O'Neal places well on the honorable mentions list. Standing at 7'2", with 4 rings for the L.A. Lakers, 3-time NBA Finals MVP, and 2000 Most Valuable Player Award, he has been very impactful in his 19-year NBA career.

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

Known for his iconic hook shot, the 7'2" big man was unstoppable during his career from 1969 to 1989. He's well known for his rivalry with Hakeem Olajuwon, who was very similar to him in physique, as well as in their playstyle. In his career, he had managed to obtain a total of 6 championships, 6 MVP Awards, and the record for most points scored all-time (before LeBron James surpassed it).

Magic Johnson

Magic is one of the best and craftiest point guards to ever play in the NBA. Playing for the Los Angeles Lakers, he always finds his open teammates and makes everyone in his team better just by his playmaking and passing ability. Along with his 5 Championships, 3 Finals MVPS, and 3 MVPS, he is also regarded to a the best point guard of all time.

Larry Bird

Larry Bird, Magic's biggest rival, and one of the greatest NBA trashtalkers. He loved to talk, but his game lived up to the talking. Bird won 3 Championships, 3 Finals MVPS, and 3 consecutive MVPS. He also won the 1988 3-point shootout in the NBA All Star games.
